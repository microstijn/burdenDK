# Mixture-assumption sensitivity (the null-result gap, closed)

*Working note, 2026-06-12. The review flagged that in the large calibrated demo all
three mixture models (TU / IA / grouped CA-then-IA) produce identical numbers
because no axis/month ever has more than one contributing compound — so the mixture
layer was effectively untested and its sensitivity unknown. This builds the missing
overlap case and quantifies it. Generated by
`examples/mixture_assumption_sensitivity.jl` (read-only).*

## (1) The models do differ — once there is same-axis overlap

`k` compounds each loading the maintenance axis with active stress `x`, reporting
`E_maintenance`:

| k | x | TU | IA | grp(same code) | grp(distinct) | \|TU−IA\| |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 3 | 0.5 | 0.600 | 0.704 | 0.600 | 0.704 | 0.104 |
| 3 | 1.0 | 0.750 | 0.875 | 0.750 | 0.875 | 0.125 |
| 5 | 0.5 | 0.714 | 0.868 | 0.714 | 0.868 | 0.154 |
| 5 | 1.0 | 0.833 | 0.969 | 0.833 | 0.969 | 0.135 |

So the machinery is correct and the choice is real: **TU is the conservative upper
bound** (concentration addition), **IA the lower bound**, and **grouped CA-then-IA
sits between** them — equal to TU when the overlapping compounds share an effect
code, equal to IA when they don't. The spread reaches **~0.15 on `E_a`** (15
percentage points). The big demo's "no mixture sensitivity" is purely an artifact of
*never having overlap*, not of the models agreeing.

## (2) Propagated sensitivity is axis-weight-dependent

The same `E_a` spread moves the margin state very differently depending on which axis
the overlap is on, because the κ-rule weights are **assimilation-led**
(`w_assimilation = ½`). For *Daphnia magna* (5 compounds, TU vs IA):

| overlap axis | weight | `Q_t` (TU / IA) | `F` (TU / IA) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| assimilation | ~0.50 | 0.357 / 0.434 | 1.090 / 1.123 |
| maintenance | ~0.15 | 0.104 / 0.126 | 1.020 / 1.024 |

Mixture choice moves `Q_t` by ~**8 percentage points** and `F` by ~**3%** on
assimilation, but only ~2 pp / ~0.5% on maintenance — a **~3.5× difference** set
entirely by the κ-rule weighting.

## Upshot (reviewer-proofing)

- The mixture-assumption proxy is **bounded**: any realised impairment lies inside
  the TU–IA envelope, and the grouped default is a defensible interpolation that
  uses the empirical effect-code grouping rather than a fitted interaction.
- It **matters most for assimilation-targeting compound overlaps** and is largely
  negligible for single-compound-per-axis exposures (the common case in the current
  data).
- This both closes the flagged null-result gap (the models *do* differentiate) and
  bounds how much the choice can move the margin state, which is the quantity the
  manuscript should report when defending the grouped CA-then-IA default.
